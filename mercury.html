Mercury

Mercury is planet closest to the Sun in our solar system, making it the fastest (88 Earth-day revolutions) and exposed to extreme radiation and temperatures (although it is not the hottest planet).

Known as terrestrial (rocky) planet, Mercury's surface is covered in innumerable impact craters.

It is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon in size and gas a very thin, unbreathable exosphere, composed mainly of O2, Na, H2, He, and K.
