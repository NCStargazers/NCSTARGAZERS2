Quasars

Quasars are very luminous, over a million solar mass objects in the center of galaxies. Because of their extreme brightness, they can be seen from distant light-years away. They are detected by their high X-ray emissions and "bluer" (hotter than star) appearances.

They are also defined by gas "falling" quickly into black holes and rotating as an "accretion disk" near the event horizon. This process is maintained by the Eddington Limit.
